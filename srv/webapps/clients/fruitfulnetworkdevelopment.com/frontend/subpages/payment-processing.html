§ Payment Processing Overview

A simple, reliable payments layer that supports online donations, fiscal sponsorship workflows, and in-person transactions (POS) across farms and programs.

Why We Recommend PayPal Integration

PayPal is a widely adopted processor that makes it straightforward to accept card payments and donations online, while providing accessible reporting and payout controls. For many small organizations, it reduces setup friction and supports fast deployment of donation pages.

  • Low-friction onboarding: common vendor, familiar donor experience, quick account setup.
  • Clear transaction records: exportable data that can be mapped into bookkeeping.
  • Donor trust: recognizable checkout flows can improve completion rates.
  • Multi-site flexibility: one nonprofit account can support many designated projects under fiscal sponsorship.

Fit for Fiscal Sponsorship Programs

In a fiscal sponsorship model, donations must be processed under the sponsor nonprofit’s legal identity (e.g., EIN and processor account). A PayPal-based setup can support this by centralizing donation intake while allowing donors to express designation intent (e.g., “Farm Infrastructure Fund”) without transferring legal control away from the sponsor.

Note: Donor designations are handled as intent. The sponsor organization must retain final decision authority and publish clear policies that describe how designated funds are managed.

POS and In-Person Flexibility

PayPal can be used for in-person payment acceptance (depending on the organization’s setup and chosen tooling), which helps unify reporting across online donations and market-day sales. This is useful when projects span both digital fundraising and on-site community support.

  • Unified reporting: align market-day transactions with online contributions.
  • Scalable across vendors: consistent workflows across farms and events.
  • Optional separation: keep retail sales and charitable donations distinct when needed.

“Donation Box” Service Layer

We can implement a lightweight “donation box” module on your website(s) that routes donations to PayPal while recording donor metadata (designation intent, contact email, receipt status) in a structured way. This supports automation for tax receipts and reporting, and enables program-level summaries across sponsored projects.

  • Embeddable UI: a consistent donation widget that can be used on multiple sites.
  • Metadata capture: designation, notes, donor info (as permitted), and internal tags.
  • Receipt workflow: automate acknowledgements and maintain an auditable trail.

Contingencies and Alternatives Alongside PayPal

While PayPal is often the fastest path to launch, we plan for contingencies: policy changes, account limitations, donor preferences, or operational needs (e.g., lower fees, ACH, recurring gifts, or tighter accounting integration). The donation box module can be designed so the payment processor is interchangeable.

  • Processor fallback: ability to route payments through a secondary provider if needed.
  • Method expansion: optional support for ACH, recurring contributions, or event-based sales.
  • Accounting alignment: configurable exports and tagging to match bookkeeping categories.
  • Separation of concerns: payments handled by a processor; program data handled by your system.

Program Context

Fiscal Sponsorship Programs

A practical structure that allows mission-aligned projects and groups to fundraise compliantly under a sponsor nonprofit, while preserving donor deductibility and maintaining strong financial oversight.

What Fiscal Sponsorship Is

Fiscal sponsorship is an arrangement where a recognized 501(c)(3) nonprofit (the sponsor) accepts charitable donations and manages funds on behalf of a mission-aligned project (a sponsored project) that is not itself a standalone nonprofit. Donations are legally made to the sponsor, who retains control and responsibility for how the funds are used.

  • Donations go to the sponsor: processed under the sponsor’s legal identity and payment accounts.
  • Projects receive support: via sponsor-managed spending or documented regrants.
  • Compliance is built-in: sponsor retains oversight, reporting, and policy control.

Why It Matters for Farms and Community Projects

Many farms and community initiatives have strong public benefit goals but cannot access traditional capital or charitable fundraising on their own. Fiscal sponsorship enables structured fundraising for specific projects (infrastructure, education, conservation, or community access) while ensuring donations remain tax-deductible and properly governed.

How We Approach Fiscal Sponsorship for Clients

We treat fiscal sponsorship as both a governance model and an operational system. We help sponsors and project leads establish clear policies, predictable workflows, and clean reporting—so fundraising is simple for donors and manageable for administrators.

  1. Program Definition: Define what projects qualify, what expenses are eligible, and what documentation is required.
  2. Donor Experience: Build donation pages that clearly state the sponsor as the recipient while allowing designation intent.
  3. Fund Controls: Implement tagging for restricted/designated funds, approval workflows, and audit-ready records.
  4. Disbursement Pathways: Support direct vendor payment or reimbursement/grants with documentation and standardized reporting.
  5. Back-End Integration: Export or sync donation and expense data into bookkeeping categories and board-level dashboards.

Governance and Transparency

A strong fiscal sponsorship program depends on clarity: donors understand who they are giving to, projects understand how funds are managed, and sponsors maintain consistent oversight. We help clients publish the required language, adopt internal policies, and maintain reporting that is appropriate for boards, auditors, and funders.

Key principle: Donors may express intent, but the sponsor must retain final decision authority and ensure all spending aligns with charitable purpose and sponsor policies.

Supporting Client Groups and Networks

When multiple farms or project teams operate under one sponsor, we structure the program so each project can fundraise under a shared system while still producing clean, separated reporting. This allows coordinated fundraising across a network without losing financial accountability.

  • Per-project pages: each project has its own public page and donation designations.
  • Per-project reporting: summaries by project, donor designations, and spending categories.
  • Consistent controls: the sponsor’s rules apply uniformly across all projects.

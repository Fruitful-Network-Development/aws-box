Novel Technology: The Mycite Framework

Stewardship for decentralized information

Problem: The failure of ownership-based information structures is one of coordination, not technology, due to information's natural properties (recombinability, resistance to scarcity, entropic spread).

Solution: The Mycite Framework is a design paradigm for stewardship, proposing decentralized, contribution-based information management without central control.

Semantic Architecture

Principles that keep data interpretable and portable

ULI Notation Delimiters

Information notation requires conventions to define where a Universal Language of Information (ULI) notation ends (self-terminating).

Information is "Retrodeterministic," meaning the full context and meaning of a part can only be understood when the notation signals it is finished.

Polymorphic referencing in data, if not managed carefully, can lead to an explosive and unmanageable size of required symbols/references.

The Mycite Framework uses specific conventions to avoid the "explosive size" problem in notating complex abstractions of rearrangement.

The Semantic Internet

Current Issue: Systems lack indiscriminate interoperability because there is no universal data standard capable of expressing the infinite variety of data structures, contexts, and behaviors.

Consequence: Software interfaces are rigid and cannot automatically reconfigure for different modes of interaction, limiting accessibility.

The Technical Core: The Mycite Framework achieves a practical, large-scale semantic data structure by integrating data and metadata into a unified system using a highly reduced alphabet of elements and SSID Layering.

Mycelium Schema Standardization (MSS): MSS reduces information to a finite set of components so encodings can be represented as navigable locations in memory, speeding development and interoperability.

Globaslim & Autocracy

Centralization's Failure: Centralized platforms create artificial drag, waste, and incompatible standards, limiting coordination for small farms.

Semantic Solution: A semantic architecture provides a shared grammar that enables cooperation without centralized control.

Barcelona Proof-Point: The Fab City initiative demonstrates a decentralized, locally productive economy by pairing global design with local fabrication.

The Mycelium Network: Unlike blockchain, the Mycite Network achieves distributed cooperation through semantic alignment—a lattice of meaning—while maintaining local autonomy.
